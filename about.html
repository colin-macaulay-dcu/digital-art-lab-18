What is the DAL?

A Lab for — Creative Bravery

The Digital Art Lab is a resource and inspiration platform for Irish primary teachers — where traditional art-making and digital technology come together. Whether you're starting with a paintbrush or an iPad, the DAL supports you to experiment and discover.

Our Story

The Digital Art Lab

Digital technology is always at the centre of the DAL. Sometimes you'll start with paint or clay and bring ideas into a digital tool. Sometimes it's purely digital. What matters is the spirit of experimentation — the willingness to try something, see what happens, and try again.

Everything here comes from real workshops with trainee and qualified teachers — experimenting together, taking chances, making mistakes, and discovering what genuinely works in a primary classroom.

The DAL is not a finished product. It is a living, growing platform — shaped by teacher feedback, workshop experiments, and the ongoing PhD research it is built on.

Digital First

Apps, drawing tools, animation, photography, video — digital technology is always at the heart of the DAL. Every resource embeds a digital element, whatever direction you take.

🖌️

Starting Physical, Going Digital

Sometimes the best starting point is a brush, a print, or collage — but the journey always leads into digital territory. Physical materials spark the idea; technology carries it further.

🔀

Hybrid Practice

Traditional and digital processes woven together — responding to each other, creating something richer than either could produce alone.

🎯

Always Curriculum-Linked

All DAL resources connect to the Irish Primary Curriculum — Visual Arts strands, competencies, and class levels clearly signposted throughout.
